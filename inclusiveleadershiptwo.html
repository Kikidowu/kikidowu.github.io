    What are these Six Cs/Values of Inclusive Leadership

    Please note that there are so many Leadership values that you can embody to be an exceptional leader. Values are often a reflection of the culture and context you find yourself in. Feel free to pick and choose anyone of these that align with your sphere of influence and/or organizational culture.

    The 6Cs of inclusive leadership provide a comprehensive framework for leaders to develop and practice inclusive behaviors, cultivate diverse and inclusive environments, and drive positive change. These six Cs—Cognizance, Courage, Curiosity, Cultural Intelligence, Collaboration, and Commitment—highlight key attributes and actions that inclusive leaders embody.

    Six Cs

    1. Curiousity
    2. Courage
    3. Cultural Intelligence
    4. Cognizance
    5. Collaboration
    6. Commitment

    Curiousity

    Inclusive leaders exhibit curiosity by seeking out different perspectives and actively listening to others. They ask questions, engage in dialogue, and genuinely try to understand the experiences and viewpoints of individuals from diverse backgrounds. Their curiosity drives them to continuously learn and broaden their understanding of different cultures, identities, and perspectives.

    Courage

    Inclusive leaders demonstrate courage by advocating for inclusion and challenging the status quo. They are not afraid to speak up against discrimination or exclusionary practices. They take risks, even when its uncomfortable, to create a more inclusive environment and ensure that diverse voices are heard and valued.

    Cultural Intelligence

    Inclusive leaders develop cultural intelligence, which involves the ability to work effectively across diverse cultures. They are sensitive to cultural nuances, respect diverse customs, and adapt their leadership style to accommodate different needs. By cultivating cultural intelligence, they create a welcoming and inclusive environment for individuals from various backgrounds.

    Cognizance

    Inclusive leaders possess cognitive awareness, which involves recognizing and challenging their own biases and assumptions. They understand the impact of unconscious biases and actively work to mitigate them. By fostering self-awareness, they create an environment that values diversity and promotes fairness and inclusivity.

    Collaboration

    Inclusive leaders prioritize collaboration and teamwork. They understand the value of diverse teams and actively encourage participation from all members. They foster an environment where diverse perspectives are welcomed, and differences are seen as strengths. By promoting collaboration, they harness the collective intelligence of their teams and drive innovation.

    Commitment

    Inclusive leaders are committed to fostering diversity, equity, and inclusion. They make it a priority in their decision-making processes, policies, and practices. They advocate for equal opportunities, address systemic barriers, and hold themselves accountable for creating inclusive environments. Their commitment inspires others and drives positive change within organizations and society.

    By embodying these 6Cs, leaders can cultivate inclusive cultures that celebrate diversity, promote fairness, and unlock the full potential of their teams. Inclusive leadership goes beyond good intentions—it requires conscious efforts, continuous learning, and a genuine commitment to building inclusive organizations and communities.
